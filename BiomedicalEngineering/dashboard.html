Patient Monitors

A patient monitor is a medical device that is used to continuously monitor a patient's vital signs. Vital signs are the basic body functions that are necessary for life. They include heart rate, blood pressure, respiratory rate, and temperature. Patient monitors are used in a variety of healthcare settings, including hospitals, clinics, and ambulances.There are many different types of patient monitors, but they all have the same basic function: to collect and display data about a patient's vital signs.

Infusion Pumps

An infusion pump is a medical device that delivers fluids, such as nutrients and medications, into a patient's body in controlled amounts. Infusion pumps are used in a variety of healthcare settings, including hospitals, clinics, and ambulances. There are many different types of infusion pumps, but they all have the same basic function: to deliver fluids at a precise rate. This is important because some medications or nutrients can be dangerous if they are delivered too quickly or in too large of a volume.

Ventillations

A ventilation monitor is a medical device that is used to continuously monitor a patient's breathing. It measures and displays important respiratory parameters, such as tidal volume, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation levels. Ventilation monitors are used in a variety of healthcare settings, including hospitals, clinics, and ambulances. These monitors are used in hospitals and clinics to monitor patients who are not critically ill. They typically display tidal volume, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation levels, and end-tidal CO2 (EtCO2) levels.

Imaging equipment

X-rays use electromagnetic radiation to create images of bones and dense tissues. They are commonly used to diagnose fractures, tumors, and other abnormalities.CT scans use X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional images of the body. They are often used to diagnose tumors, infections, and other abnormalities in the body.MRIs use magnetic fields and radio waves to create detailed images of the body's soft tissues, such as muscles, ligaments, and organs.
